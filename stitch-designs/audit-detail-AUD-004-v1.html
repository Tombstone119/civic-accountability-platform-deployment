Executive Summary

This forensic investigation was triggered by internal anomalies detected in the procurement process for the 2024 IT Infrastructure Upgrade. The primary focus of this audit is to verify compliance with the Civic Accountability Framework and to evaluate the value-for-money metrics of the hardware acquisitions. Initial findings indicate several deviations from standard operating procedures, specifically regarding pricing transparency and contractual performance guarantees.

Scope of Work

Full review of direct award documentation, unit pricing verification, and service level agreement (SLA) benchmarking for Project AURORA.

Period Covered

January 2024 – June 2024. Includes all purchase orders under the initial upgrade phase.

Detailed Findings

Ref Finding & Description Severity Category
F-01

Pricing Discrepancy (Server Hardware)

Unit pricing for high-density servers exceeds regional market rates by 34% without technical justification.

 Critical Financial Waste
F-02

Incomplete Direct Award Documentation

The mandatory 'Unique Vendor Justification' form was missing from the procurement file for CON-2024-001.

 High Compliance Gap
F-03

Absence of Performance Benchmarks

Initial contract lacks defined KPIs or uptime guarantees, limiting departmental recourse in event of failure.

 Medium Risk Mitigation

Immediate Recommendations

1

Freeze Payments

Suspend pending disbursements to the vendor until hardware unit pricing is renegotiated or justified in writing by the vendor management team.

2

Retrospective Justification

Direct the procurement officer to provide the missing documentation for the direct award process within 48 business hours.

3

Contract Addendum

Draft an amendment to CON-2024-001 including industry-standard SLAs and liquidated damage clauses for all upcoming phases of Project AURORA.
